<p><p>PH and ORP sensors are instrumentation devices used to measure the acidity or alkalinity (pH) and oxidation-reduction potential (ORP) of liquid solutions. These sensors play a crucial role in various industries including water treatment, pharmaceutical, food and beverage, and environmental monitoring.</p><p>The pH sensor measures the concentration of hydrogen ions in a solution, indicating its acidity or alkalinity on a scale of 0 to 14. On the other hand, the ORP sensor measures the ability of a solution to oxidize or reduce other substances, providing insights into its disinfection capacity or potential.</p><p>The global market for pH and ORP sensors is witnessing steady growth due to increasing demand for water and wastewater treatment applications, alongside stringent regulations to maintain water quality. <p><p>The PH and ORP sensors market includes two main types of sensors: insertion and immersion sensors. Insertion sensors are designed to be inserted into a process stream or tank for continuous monitoring of pH or oxidation-reduction potential (ORP). These sensors provide accurate and real-time measurements, making them ideal for industrial applications. On the other hand, immersion sensors are submerged directly into a sample for shorter periods, mainly for laboratory or field analysis. These sensors offer quick and convenient measurements and are commonly used in water treatment and environmental monitoring applications.</p></p>
